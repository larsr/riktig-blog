riktig #21 — Backprop is associativity

Twenty-first post documenting the riktig project. The linear algebra course meets the analysis course, and the punchline is that two famous algorithms are the same theorem, bracketed two different ways.

What we are doing, and why. Verified software in Coq, built for modifiability. Post #18 named the goal precisely: get transformers, flash attention, and diffusion models into reach. Today’s post is the first payoff of that plan — the fact that makes automatic differentiation work at all, stated and proved in a form short enough to fit in this paragraph’s neighborhood.

The question

A transformer, or any layered network, is a chain of differentiable functions: f3∘f2∘f1f_3 \circ f_2 \circ f_1. The chain rule says the derivative of the composite is the product of the derivatives — a product of matrices (Jacobians), once a basis is chosen:

D(f3∘f2∘f1)(a)=J3⋅J2⋅J1.D(f_3 \circ f_2 \circ f_1)(a) \;=\; J_3 \cdot J_2 \cdot J_1.

Two famous algorithms compute this product by grouping the multiplications differently:

  • Forward-mode AD seeds a tangent vector vv at the input and pushes it forward: J1vJ_1 v, then J2(J1v)J_2(J_1 v), then J3(J2(J1v))J_3(J_2(J_1 v)) — the running product grows on the left, bracketed J3⋅(J2⋅J1)J_3 \cdot (J_2 \cdot J_1).
  • Reverse-mode AD — backprop seeds a cotangent ww at the output and pulls it backward: wJ3w J_3, then (wJ3)J2(w J_3) J_2, then ((wJ3)J2)J1((w J_3) J_2) J_1 — the running product grows on the right, bracketed (J3⋅J2)⋅J1(J_3 \cdot J_2) \cdot J_1.

Every course states this and moves on to the efficiency argument (why reverse-mode is cheap for scalar losses). The thing that’s usually asserted rather than shown is the easy half: that both bracketings give the same matrix in the first place. That’s the theorem this post proves — and it turns out to need almost nothing beyond a fact you already know: matrix multiplication is associative.

The proof, twice

At the level of linear maps, composition (lm_comp) is just function composition — fun u => g (f u). Any two ways of bracketing three composed functions beta-reduce to the identical function. So the general fact is reflexivity:

Theorem lm_comp_assoc : forall (g : LinearMap F R S) (h : LinearMap F Q R)
    (k : LinearMap F P Q),
  lm_comp (lm_comp g h) k == lm_comp g (lm_comp h k).
Proof.
  intros g h k u.
  reflexivity.
Qed.

Post #14 (well — the linear algebra track generally) already proved that mat_of, the map from a LinearMap to its matrix in a chosen basis, is a Proper morphism: equal linear maps get equal matrices. Feed lm_comp_assoc through that morphism and the headline theorem — the one about actual differentiable functions, actual is_deriv witnesses, actual Jacobians — is one line:

Theorem jacobian_chain3_assoc :
  forall (f1 : U -> V) (f2 : V -> W) (f3 : W -> X) (a : U)
    (L1 : LinearMap F U V) (L2 : LinearMap F V W) (L3 : LinearMap F W X)
    (P2 : Proper (equ ==> equ) f2) (P3 : Proper (equ ==> equ) f3)
    (Hd1 : is_deriv f1 a L1) (Hd2 : is_deriv f2 (f1 a) L2)
    (Hd3 : is_deriv f3 (f2 (f1 a)) L3),
  jacobian n q BU BX
    (deriv_comp f1 (fun v => f3 (f2 v)) a L1 (lm_comp L3 L2) _ Hd1
       (deriv_comp f2 f3 (f1 a) L2 L3 P3 Hd2 Hd3))
  == jacobian n q BU BX
    (deriv_comp (fun u => f2 (f1 u)) f3 a (lm_comp L2 L1) L3 P3
       (deriv_comp f1 f2 a L1 L2 P2 Hd1 Hd2) Hd3).
Proof.
  exact (mat_of_proper BU BX (lm_comp (lm_comp L3 L2) L1)
           (lm_comp L3 (lm_comp L2 L1)) (lm_comp_assoc L3 L2 L1)).
Qed.

The two sides are the Jacobian computed by composing the chain rule one way, versus the other. deriv_comp is post #12’s chain rule (the derivative is a linear map); everything downstream of it — the Jacobian, the matrix product, this theorem — is bookkeeping around that one fact. Named corollaries unpack each side as an explicit matrix product, so the two algorithms are visible by name:

Theorem jacobian_reverse_mode : ... ==
  mat_mul (mat_mul (jacobian ... Hd3) (jacobian ... Hd2)) (jacobian ... Hd1).

Theorem jacobian_forward_mode : ... ==
  mat_mul (jacobian ... Hd3) (mat_mul (jacobian ... Hd2) (jacobian ... Hd1)).

and a third theorem, backprop_eq_forward_mode, restates the whole thing purely as mat_mul_assoc at the Jacobian level — confirming that no new mathematics was smuggled in anywhere.

Getting the direction right by deriving it, not assuming it

Post #18’s stage-6½ entry on the analysis roadmap had already sketched this theorem — and had the bracketing direction backwards. It’s an easy mistake: “forward” and “backward” are directions of data flow, not directions of association, and it’s not obvious in advance which maps to which without actually tracing through what each algorithm computes. Rather than trust the earlier prose, the proof works it out from the cost argument directly: reverse-mode’s running product starts at the output-side factor and grows by extending on the right toward the input, which is the left-associated bracketing (J3⋅J2)⋅J1(J_3 \cdot J_2) \cdot J_1; forward-mode’s running product starts at the input-side factor and grows by extending on the left toward the output, the right-associated bracketing J3⋅(J2⋅J1)J_3 \cdot (J_2 \cdot J_1). The roadmap entry has been corrected to match. Small thing, but it’s the kind of small thing that’s easy to get backwards in a textbook and hard to get backwards in a proof — the proof forces you to actually pin down which product is which.

What this doesn’t yet claim

Two honest limits, stated rather than hidden:

  • The file proves the N=3N=3 and N=4N=4 cases explicitly, and shows by construction that N=4N=4’s proof is just two applications of N=3N=3’s lemma — the general NN-ary chain is the same induction one more time, left as a documented TODO rather than pushed through here. The fussy part isn’t the mathematics (associativity in any category generalizes for free); it’s indexing “the space after step ii” as a clean dependent family, which deserves its own pass.
  • This theorem is about the Jacobian, the full matrix. Real backprop never materializes it — it composes linear-map applications directly, propagating a single vector through each layer at O(dim⁡)O(\dim) cost instead of building and multiplying O(dim⁡2)O(\dim^2) or O(dim⁡3)O(\dim^3) matrices. That’s a legitimate descend-to-implementation refinement, queued for later, in the same pattern as every other spec/impl split in this project.

Why this is the right first move

docs/ML-ROADMAP.md staged the path to transformers, flash attention, and diffusion honestly by difficulty, and flagged the optimization- equivalence pattern — proving a fast implementation computes the same value as a naive spec — as the most novel part of the plan. Today’s theorem is the simplest possible instance of exactly that pattern, one level up: forward-mode and reverse-mode aren’t two different computations that happen to agree: they’re the same computation, differently grouped, and associativity is the proof. Flash attention will be a longer, harder version of the same shape of argument. Good to know the shape works before reaching for the hard case.
