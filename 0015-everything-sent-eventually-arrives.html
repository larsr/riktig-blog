riktig #15 — Everything sent eventually arrives

Fifteenth post documenting the riktig project. The channels thread closes its opening arc; the determinant, promised back in post #13, gets its coordinate-free definition.

What we are doing, and why. Verified software in Coq, built for modifiability. Post #14 proved the alternating-bit protocol never lets a corrupted or duplicated message through — safety, unconditional. Safety alone is a low bar: a protocol that discards every packet is perfectly safe. Today’s other half is liveness — does anything actually get through? — and, on the algebra side, the determinant finally means what it always should have: not a formula, but the number by which a map scales volume.

Safety was free; liveness has a price

Post #14’s theorem held for any channel behavior — total packet loss included, in which case delivery is silently empty. That’s honest but unsatisfying: you also want a guarantee that messages arrive. That guarantee cannot be unconditional — a channel that eats every packet forever really does deliver nothing, and no proof can say otherwise. So liveness needs a hypothesis, and the whole craft is finding the weakest one that’s still true of real networks.

Fixpoint fair_wire (b : bit) (ms : list M) (k : nat) (wire : list Packet) : Prop :=
  match ms with
  | [] => wire = []
  | m :: ms' =>
      exists w1 w2, wire = w1 ++ w2 /\
        lossy_dup (sender_burst b m k) w1 /\ w1 <> [] /\
        fair_wire (negb b) ms' k w2
  end.

Read the third conjunct: w1 <> [] — at least one copy of this message’s retransmission burst survives. That’s the entire fairness assumption. Everything else is unchanged: the channel can still drop some copies, duplicate others, reorder within the burst — anything lossy_dup allows — as long as it doesn’t drop all of them.

Theorem abp_liveness : forall k b0 ms wire,
  fair_wire b0 ms k wire -> deliver b0 wire = ms.

Under that one assumption, delivery is exactly the sent list — no subsequence slack, no missing messages. Compare the two theorems side by side and you can see the whole shape of the argument: abp_safety needed no assumption and delivered “a subsequence of”; abp_liveness needed “at least one copy per burst” and delivered “exactly.” The strength of the conclusion tracks the strength of the hypothesis, with nothing hidden in between — that correspondence is what a liveness proof is supposed to show you.

A companion theorem confirms the endpoint everyone actually cares about: over the perfect channel (no loss at all), delivery is exact too —

Theorem abp_collapse_perfect : forall k b0 ms wire,
  k > 0 -> perfect (sender_stream b0 ms k) wire -> deliver b0 wire = ms.

— with k > 0 earning its keep as a genuine, necessary hypothesis: with a retransmission budget of zero, nothing is ever sent, so of course nothing arrives. (Small, honest detail: abp_liveness needed no such side condition — fair_wire with k = 0 is simply unsatisfiable for a nonempty message, since an empty burst can never contain a surviving packet. The right hypothesis makes the degenerate case impossible instead of needing a bolt-on exception.)

This closes the arc the roadmap laid out: T1 gave channels a vocabulary (relations, not probabilities), T2 gave protocols a shape (state machines composed through a channel), T3 proved safety unconditionally, T4 proved liveness under the one hypothesis reality actually satisfies. The next protocols on the roadmap — Go-Back-N, selective repeat, the reliability core of TCP — inherit this whole structure; a new retransmission scheme only ever owes its own burst-delivery argument.

The determinant, defined honestly

Post #13 promised “how a map scales alternating volume” as the real definition of determinant, with the Laplace-expansion formula (Impl/Det.v) demoted to a mere computation lemma. Delivering the coordinate-free version in full generality turns out to require a fact — the space of alternating top-forms on an n-dimensional space is exactly one-dimensional — that is itself a serious theorem, not yet in the tree. Rather than fake it, this wave draws the line exactly where the honest proof runs out, and says so in the code.

What’s unconditional: precomposing an alternating form with any linear map preserves alternation —

Theorem fstar_alternating : Alternating omega -> Alternating (fstar f omega).

a clean, fully general lemma for any dimension, with no gaps.

What’s basis-relative, and proved right: reusing L2’s representation theorem directly,

Definition det (B : Basis n F U) (f : LinearMap F U U) : F := detM (mat_of B B f).

Theorem det_id : forall B, det B lm_id == 1.  (* unconditional *)

What’s conditional, and says so:

Section EndoDetConditional.
  Context (detM_mul : forall A A', detM (mat_mul A A') == detM A * detM A').

  Theorem det_basis_indep : forall B B' f, det B' f == det B f.
  Theorem det_comp : forall B f g, det B (lm_comp f g) == det B f * det B g.
End EndoDetConditional.

det_basis_indep — the theorem that makes “coordinate-free” a fact and not a slogan — follows post #13’s trace-basis-independence argument almost exactly: conjugate by a change-of-basis matrix, cancel. But cancelling needs detM to be multiplicative, and multiplicativity is the one fact this wave doesn’t yet have — proving it cleanly wants the elementary-matrix machinery of Gaussian elimination, queued for wave L5. So it enters as a Context hypothesis: a named, visible assumption sitting right in the theorem’s premises, not a silent gap and not a Coq Axiom either. When L5 lands, that hypothesis gets discharged — proved once, for real — and every theorem built on top of it becomes unconditional retroactively, for free. That’s the payoff of writing the dependency down instead of hiding it: the proof obligation is exactly one lemma, visible in the type, waiting to be paid.
