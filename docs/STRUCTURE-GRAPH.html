Structure graph — the typeclass-and-record hierarchy

The structure graph (SVG) shows the Interface/*.v hierarchy of the algebra tower. It has two kinds of node, both clickable through to their coqdoc definition:

  • Solid-bordered nodes are structural Classes — the structures instance search assembles (Monoid, Group, AbGroup, Ring, Field, VectorSpace, InnerProduct, PosInner, NormedSpace, GeometricAlgebra, the Category/Functor family, DecTotalOrder, …).
  • Dashed-bordered nodes are the data-carrying Records — LinearMap, Basis, BilinearMap, MultilinearForm, Alternating. These are declared as Records, not Classes, on purpose: a linear map is a specific function together with its proofs, a basis is a chosen set of coordinates, so instance search must never hunt for one. They still belong in the same hierarchy — a LinearMap requires a Field and two VectorSpaces, a Basis carries a LinearMap, an Alternating refines a MultilinearForm — so they are drawn here too, the dashed border marking that they are data rather than instances.

An arrow points from a structure to the one it embeds or requires (Group -> Monoid, Ring -> AbGroup, LinearMap -> VectorSpace, Basis -> LinearMap, Alternating -> MultilinearForm) — the same reading as “Group embeds Monoid” or “a Basis requires a LinearMap”.

Operational typeclasses (HasAdd, HasNeg, HasScale, EqRel, …) are deliberately absent: they carry one symbol each, not a structure, and including them would turn the picture into the same wiring diagram as the source instead of the hierarchy on top of it. The one bundle- style class left out is ScaleLaws (the four scale laws), a property mixin in the same family as Assoc/Idl in Props.v, not a structure. FormallyReal is left out for the same reason: it is a Class, but a single bare value (its sum-of-squares predicate), not a bundled structure.

What this graph does not show

This is the hierarchy of the algebra tower only. Three other tracks in the repository have no typeclass-or-record hierarchy of this shape, so there is nothing of this form to draw for them — a graph of Require dependencies would be a different, cruder artifact that would not show anything as informative as this one does for the algebra tower:

  • Lambda calculus (theories/Lambda/*.v) — terms, substitution and reduction are plain inductive datatypes and recursive functions, not structures with parents.
  • The Programs track (theories/Programs/*.v) — the sorting and searching algorithms are ordinary Fixpoints with correctness proofs. They are built on the decidable total order in Interface/DecTotalOrder.v (which, being a Class, does appear as a node above), but the programs themselves form no hierarchy.
  • The Channel track (theories/Channel/*.v) — likewise plain inductive datatypes and recursive functions.

To follow those tracks, read the blog: its syllabus now has a dedicated post for each of them.
