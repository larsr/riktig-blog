Geometric algebra roadmap — wedge, dot, blades, rotors, and a robot

Written 2026-07-19 from Lars’s request (docs/TODO.md task 3, quoted below) and a full survey of the existing GA files. This is the reference document for the track; read it before touching any GA file.

The request (Lars, verbatim, 2026-07-18)

I looked at the formalization of Geometric Algebra and it didn’t quite look like i expected. When i have read about geometric algebra it has shown properties about the wedge and dot product and how they can define the geometric product. Perhaps one can define it in more ways, but at least we should be able to show that they are equivalent. And then there has been proofs that multiplying the wedge product of different base vectors become multi-vectors or blades or what’s it called. and how the geometric algebra vectors are a 2^d dimensional vector field constructed on an underlying d dimensional vector space. And how the geometric 2d space is isomorphic to the complex numbers. And then the inner product was tied into the norm and the rules about how to rotate vectors by rotors. Please create a blog post of these things, first in regular notation, and then a corresponding coq implementation of the same calculations. And then we can write a program that takes a rigid body of straight beams and connections that can either rotate or fold the joint 360 deg (without gimble lock problems), and with geometric algebra it becomes easy to chain them together to model a robot so we can compute the position of its limbs as a function of the steering angles.

What exists today (survey 2026-07-19)

  • Interface/GeometricAlgebra.v: the axiomatic presentation — an associative unital F-algebra A, a linear embedding ga_vec : V → A, and the single contraction axiom ga_vec u * ga_vec u == norm2 u *: 1. Deliberately order-free (works in any characteristic).
  • Theory/GeometricAlgebra.v: polarization (the symmetric part of uv is the doubled inner product), contract_add, vec_zero.
  • Theory/Exterior.v: wedge u v := uv − vu (the antisymmetric part), wedge_self_zero, wedge_anti, and the DOUBLED decomposition 2·uv == 2(u·v)·1 + u∧v (a bare field cannot halve); plus the separate omega_B top-form/determinant section.
  • Impl/CliffordFin.v: two fully-applied instances — GA_trivial (V=A=F) and GA_Cliff1 = Cl(0,1) with carrier C F (i² = −1). No Fin.t d-indexed 2^d construction.
  • Impl/Complex.v: the full C F stack (C_Ring for any field, C_Field under FormallyReal); no GA≅C isomorphism stated.
  • Inner product ↔︎ norm: fully formalized (norm2 u := ⟨u,u⟩, ga_contract, polarization).
  • Rotors, reflections, blades, grades, even subalgebras: absent.

Design positions

  1. Two presentations, one equivalence. The tree keeps the order-free contraction axiom as the primitive Interface (its generality is deliberate). The wedge/dot-first presentation is a Theory-level structure, with the equivalence proved in both directions and its asymmetry stated plainly: wedge/dot-first implies contraction over ANY field (take u = v; the alternating law kills the wedge term), while contraction implies wedge/dot only when 2 is invertible (the halving lives in Theory/Order.v). Alternating (w u u == 0), not antisymmetry, is the primitive on the wedge side — antisymmetry follows, and the converse fails in characteristic 2.
  2. Rotors without trigonometry. A rotor in Cl(2,0) is a unit even element — parameterize by (c, s) with c² + s² == 1 instead of cos/sin. Everything stays algebraic over a FormallyReal field; no analysis is needed for any theorem on the wish list. Angles enter only as (c,s) pairs; composition of rotors is the angle-addition formula, for free, as complex multiplication in the even subalgebra.
  3. The 2^d construction is its own project. A general Cl(d) over Fin.t d -> F (carrier: blade coefficients indexed by subsets of basis indices; product with sign bookkeeping; associativity is the hard combinatorial proof) is scheduled as a separate designed wave, NOT folded into the concrete-instance work. The concrete Cl(2) unblocks rotors and ≅ℂ without it.

Physics extension (2026-07-20, Lars’s request)

Lars watched a video explaining spin/Pauli via geometric algebra, found the standard treatment “ad hoc” by comparison, and asked for (1) a post on how symmetry groups embed in GA, (2) a follow-up post using that to explain quarks/gluons/electrons, (3) a post on Maxwell’s four equations as one GA equation. Web research was done first — see the four-topic survey this session ran (Maxwell/GA, spin-Pauli/GA, SU(3)/Standard-Model-in-GA, and the pedagogical-complaint genre the video likely belongs to). The findings split sharply by how settled each piece is, and that split governs everything below — never present the SU(3)/full-Standard-Model material as if it had the same status as U(1)/SU(2)/Maxwell.

What’s settled (textbook math, safe to formalize and blog as fact)

  • U(1): the unit even subalgebra of Cl(2,0) — Wave 2 above.
  • SU(2) / Spin(3): the even subalgebra of Cl(3,0) is isomorphic to the quaternions; its unit elements form Spin(3) ≅ SU(2); the map R ↦ (a ↦ R a R̃) is the 2-to-1 covering homomorphism onto SO(3) (R and −R act identically on vectors but oppositely on spinors — the 720°/double-cover fact, the “plate trick”). Textbook: Hestenes; Doran & Lasenby, Geometric Algebra for Physicists (CUP
    1. ch. 2, 8; Lounesto, Clifford Algebras and Spinors (2001).
  • The Pauli-matrix correspondence: Cl(3,0) ≅ Mat(2,ℂ); under e_k ↦ σ_k, the pseudoscalar I = e1e2e3 is central (odd dimension ⇒ commutes with everything) and I² = −1 — I plays i’s algebraic role as a genuine geometric object (an oriented volume), not a postulated symbol. A nonrelativistic spinor is a real even multivector ψ = √ρ · R (Hestenes 1967/1984), reproducing the Pauli equation without complex numbers.
  • Maxwell’s equations: spacetime algebra Cl(1,3) (or Cl(3,1) — a labeling choice, not a disagreement), the field bivector F = E + IcB, the vector derivative ∇ = γ^μ ∂_μ, the four-current J, and the single equation ∇F = J. Since ∇ has grade 1 and F grade 2, ∇F = ∇·F + ∇∧F splits into a grade-1 part (packaging Gauss’s law + Ampère–Maxwell) and a grade-3 part (packaging no-monopoles + Faraday) — this is uncontested algebraic bookkeeping, not a new physical claim, and the standard textbook treatment (Hestenes 1966/2003; Doran & Lasenby ch. 5–7; Dressel, Bliokh & Nori, Phys. Rep. 589 (2015) 1–72 for a modern rederivation).

What GA does NOT explain — must not be claimed

  • Pauli exclusion (antisymmetry of the fermion wavefunction under exchange) is not derived from Cl(3,0)/rotor structure. Its accepted derivation is the relativistic spin-statistics theorem (Fierz 1939, Pauli 1940 — a QFT result from locality + Lorentz invariance + positive energy, unrelated to 3D geometric algebra). A narrower thread (Doran–Lasenby–Gull’s multiparticle spacetime algebra) encodes the antisymmetry postulate compactly — that is a representational convenience, not a first-principles derivation of why fermions antisymmetrize. The blog post may say GA makes spin’s geometry transparent and removes the need for an unmotivated i; it must not say GA explains exclusion.

What’s an open, non-consensus research program (survey honestly, endorse nothing)

Getting SU(3), or the full SU(3)×SU(2)×U(1), out of a single Clifford/geometric algebra is not settled physics — it is a small cluster of competing, mutually-citing single-paper proposals, none mainstream, none uniquely reproducing the correct three-generation structure without extra hand-added ingredients: - Trayling & Baylis, J. Phys. A 34 (2001) 3309 (Cl(7,0), one generation, predicts bare sin²θ_W = 0.25). - Furey, Eur. Phys. J. C 78 (2018) 375 (division-algebra ladder operators on ℂ⊗ℍ⊗𝕆, one generation, same sin²θ_W prediction). - Stoica, Adv. Appl. Clifford Algebras 28 (2018) 52 (complex Cl6). - Daviau, Adv. Appl. Clifford Algebras 27 (2017) 279 (Cl(1,5)). - Gillard & Gresnigt and later Gourlay & Gresnigt (2019–2024): sedenion / Cl(8) extensions reaching for three generations, adding an S3 family symmetry by hand to fix earlier versions. - A published critique exists (Rowlands & Rowlands, J. Phys. Conf. Ser. 1251 (2019) 012044) questioning whether non-associative octonions do any work beyond their associative “shadow” operators.

The one piece of genuinely uncontested pure math beyond U(1)/SU(2) is: Aut(𝕆) = G2 (octonion automorphisms), and the subgroup fixing one unit imaginary octonion is exactly SU(3) (Günaydin & Gürsey, J. Math. Phys. 14 (1973) 1651; canonical write-up: Baez, Bull. Amer. Math. Soc. 39 (2002) 145, arXiv:math/0105155). This is real, checkable, and — critically — Baez himself, presenting it, explicitly declines to claim physical significance (“Maybe this is all just a coincidence. Maybe not!”). Octonions are non-associative and not a Clifford algebra (only closely related); formalizing this fact well is its own undertaking (non-associativity breaks most of Coq’s ring/field automation) and is scoped as a possible future wave, not blocking the physics posts below.

Decision: do not formalize or blog any full-unification claim as settled. Impl/Cliff3.v (Wave 3, dispatched) covers only the solid SU(2)/spin content. A separate, small Theory/SU3Algebra.v (Wave 5) formalizes the abstract su(3) Lie algebra (Gell-Mann-style generators and structure constants) on its own terms — real, uncontested finite algebra, no GA embedding claimed — so the quarks/gluons post can explain color charge honestly via standard representation theory, with the GA-embedding question surveyed in prose (attributed, not endorsed) rather than claimed as a theorem.
