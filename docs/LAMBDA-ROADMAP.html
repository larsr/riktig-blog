Track FO — the first-order form (task 1)

The request, condensed: convert a lambda term into a form where every function call is variable(variables) — function position and every argument are let-bound variables introduced higher up — via a pipeline of node-wise rewrites (closure conversion: no free variables inside any function, an explicit context/environment argument instead; every function takes all its arguments), landing 1-to-1 in a datatype that makes any other call shape unrepresentable, ready for machine-code generation. A small set of primitives survives as opaque operators (read/write a word at an address, word arithmetic); every source lambda disappears.

Survey facts that shape the plan (2026-07-19): - Syntax.v’s tm is pure de Bruijn (Var/Abs/App), no primitive or constant constructor; primitives therefore enter only in the new target datatype (SKI’s inert CVar is the precedent for “opaque, no reduction rule”). - ANF.v names every intermediate (let-telescopes, atoms in call positions) but its atoms include Abs, and its grammar is Prop predicates over loose tm, not an enforcing datatype. Its header proves why an unconditional anf t == t is false under CBV — the general correctness statement must be a simulation, not a conv fact. - Defunc.v is the closure-representation archetype (constructor per function shape + apply dispatcher) but hardcoded to one program; no free-variable analysis exists anywhere in the track. - Pipeline order: closure-convert / lambda-lift FIRST, ANF-normalize LAST (closure conversion introduces new applications — env packing and projections — that the final ANF pass then flattens). - Namespaces: do not overload one de Bruijn index space. The target separates top-level function names (a stable table, de Bruijn LEVELS), environment slots, and let-locals. This is where the survey flags the main indexing hazard; expect the correctness proof’s difficulty to concentrate in one substitution/shift commutation lemma, as in SKI.v and CPS.v.

Track DS — delayed substitution as a composable algebra (task 2)

The paper is identified: “Rebound: Efficient, Expressive, and Well-Scoped Binding”, Noé De Santo & Stephanie Weirich, Haskell Symposium 2025 (arXiv:2509.13261; github.com/sweirich/rebound; the lambda-n-ways benchmark line). The mechanism: binders carry a suspended parallel substitution (Bind : Env m n -> Exp (S m) -> Bind n); substitution pushed through a term STOPS at each binder and COMPOSES into the suspension instead of traversing; the environment is a defunctionalized inductive (Inc k | Cons t r | r :<> s) whose smart-constructor composition implements the λσ equations as oriented rewrites — including the literal cancellation comp (Inc (S k)) (Cons t p) = comp (Inc k) p, where a pending substitution entry is discarded dead, exactly the “two substitutions cancel” recollection. Variable resolution against the piled-up composite is ONE applyEnv lookup. The fusion law applyE s ∘ applyE r = applyE (r ∘ s) is the map-fusion shape (map f ∘ map g = map (f ∘ g)) for a monoid/category of pending work acting on a syntax — Lars’s Traversable intuition, made exact: scopes are objects, environments are morphisms, terms are a module over that category.
